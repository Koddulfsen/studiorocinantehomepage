# Template System

## Framework

Two layers that combine to define each template:

**Archetype** — what the site is selling you on  
**Subcategory** — the visual language it speaks

---

## Archetypes

| # | Archetype | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| A | Food Closeup | The dish is the hero. Everything steps aside. |
| B | Atmosphere | Selling the feeling of being in the room. |
| C | Story | Founder, heritage, origin. Builds loyalty. |
| D | Bold Graphic | The design IS the vibe. Photos optional. |
| E | Minimal | Restraint and white space. Let the brand breathe. |

---

## Subcategories

| # | Name | Personality |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Noir | Dark background, white type, moody. Upscale, a little mysterious. |
| 2 | Broadsheet | Editorial newspaper energy. Typographic, barely needs photos. |
| 3 | Polaroid | Film grain grid. Makes phone photos look intentional. |
| 4 | Market | Earthy, hand-drawn feel. Honest and local. |
| 5 | Neon | Bold color blocks, poster energy. Attitude over photos. |
| 6 | Manor | Spacious serif type, white space. Serious without being cold. |
| 7 | Corner | "Been on this street 20 years." Neighborhood institution energy. |
| 8 | Marquee | The name is the entire hero. Giant type, full screen. |

---

## Photo Dependency

**Photo-forgiving** (design carries the page): Noir, Broadsheet, Neon, Manor, Corner, Marquee  
**Fixes bad photos** (style makes them look intentional): Polaroid  
**Needs decent photos**: Market, Food Closeup combos

---

## Outreach Workflow

1. Find business on Google Maps with no/bad website
2. Pull their photos, name, cuisine, neighborhood
3. Pick 3 fitting templates from the library
4. Generate preview pages at `studiorocinante.com/[business-slug]`
5. Screenshot each → send cold email with images + live links
